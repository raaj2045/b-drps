For each paper, only its metadata is stored on-chain — the author, title,
abstract, and review comments, together with the IPFS content identifier
(CID) that references the manuscript, which is stored off-chain — so each
paper occupies a fixed amount of storage (approximately 3.0 KiB, of which
six copies are retained across the pipeline's archival arrays) and total
storage grows linearly with the number of papers, independent of
manuscript size. The slot-level accounting underlying the figure was
validated against an SSTORE count obtained via debug_traceTransaction,
with exact agreement (123 = 123 slots per publication lifecycle).
