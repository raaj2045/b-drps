Gas consumption is adopted as the scalability metric because it is
deterministic across execution environments (identical per-operation values
were observed on the local network and on forked Sepolia state) and because
both transaction fees and achievable throughput derive from it, making it
the only measure that transfers unchanged to a production deployment. The
results indicate constant per-unit cost at scale: mean gas per paper fits
mean(N) = 4,817,250 + 139,888/N to within 0.004%, corresponding to a
constant marginal cost with a one-time storage-initialization premium on
the first submission (EIP-2200), while per-operation gas remains invariant
(0.0% variation) as pre-existing state grows from 10 to 5,000 entries.
