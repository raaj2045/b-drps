Gas is the EVM's deterministic unit of work — identical on a local node,
testnet, and mainnet (verified byte-for-byte in gas.csv) — so it is the only
scalability metric that transfers to a real deployment unchanged, and fees
and throughput both derive from it. The figure shows cost is flat at scale:
every paper after the first costs the same 4,817,250 gas (the measured means
fit mean(N) = 4,817,250 + 139,888/N to within 0.004%, the first paper paying
a one-time zero-slot initialization premium per EIP-2200), and per-operation
gas is exactly O(1) (0.0% variation) as pre-existing state grows from 10 to
5,000 entries.
